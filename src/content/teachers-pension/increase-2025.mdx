## How the Increase Works

### For Retired Teachers

If you're already receiving your Teachers' Pension:

1. The increase is applied automatically
2. Your April 2025 payment will be at the new rate
3. No action is needed from you

### For Current Teachers

If you're still teaching and building pension:

- Your **accrued benefits** are revalued by **CPI + 1.6%**
- This is 3.3% for the 2024/25 year
- Applied to all pension you've built up so far

<Callout type="info" title="Two Types of Increase">
- **Retired teachers**: Pension increases by CPI (1.7%)
- **Working teachers**: Accrued pension revalued by CPI + 1.6% (3.3%)
</Callout>

## When Will You See the Increase?

### Payment Dates

- The increase applies from **April 2025**
- Your first increased payment depends on your payment date
- Most pensioners will see the increase in their April or May payment

### Checking Your New Amount

1. Login to My Pension Online
2. Check your pension record
3. Or wait for your April payslip showing the new amount

## CPI vs RPI

The Teachers' Pension uses **CPI** (Consumer Prices Index) for increases, not RPI (Retail Prices Index).

<Callout type="info" title="Why CPI?">
CPI typically produces lower increases than RPI. The government switched public sector pensions to CPI in 2011 as a cost-saving measure.
</Callout>

## Revaluation for Working Teachers

If you're still teaching, your pension benefits are revalued differently:

### Career Average (2015 Scheme)

- Revaluation rate: **CPI + 1.6%**
- For 2024/25: 1.7% + 1.6% = **3.3%**
- Applied to all pension built up before this year
