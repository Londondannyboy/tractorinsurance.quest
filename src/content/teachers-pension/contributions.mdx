## How Your Contribution is Calculated

### Step 1: Identify Your Pensionable Salary

**Pensionable salary** typically includes:

- Basic salary
- Teaching and Learning Responsibility (TLR) payments
- Special Educational Needs (SEN) allowances
- Other contractual allowances

**Usually not included**:

- Overtime (unless contractual)
- Expenses
- Performance bonuses

<Callout type="info" title="Check Your Payslip">
Your payslip shows your "pensionable pay". This is used to calculate your contribution, which may differ from gross pay.
</Callout>

### Step 2: Find Your Contribution Tier

Look at the table above to find which tier your annual pensionable salary falls into.

### Step 3: Calculate Your Contribution

Multiply your pensionable salary by your tier's percentage rate.

## Tax Relief on Contributions

You receive **tax relief** on your Teachers' Pension contributions, meaning your pension costs less than the headline rate.

### How Tax Relief Works

Contributions are taken from your pay **before** income tax is calculated. This gives automatic tax relief.

## Part-Time Teachers

If you work **part-time**, your contribution rate is based on your **actual earnings**, not the full-time equivalent.

<Callout type="example" title="Part-Time Example">
**Sarah** works 3 days per week (0.6 FTE). Full-time salary would be Â£40,000, but she earns Â£24,000.

Her contribution tier is based on **Â£24,000** (Tier 1 at 7.4%), not Â£40,000.

This means part-time teachers often pay a lower percentage than full-time colleagues on the same scale.
</Callout>

## Multiple Teaching Jobs

If you have more than one teaching job:

- Each job has its own contribution rate
- Rates based on each job's salary separately
- All jobs count towards your total pension

<Callout type="info" title="Multiple Jobs">
Combined earnings don't affect individual job contribution rates. Each is calculated separately based on that job's salary.
</Callout>

## Supply Teachers

Supply teachers can join the Teachers' Pension Scheme:

- Must be employed by a scheme employer (school, agency)
- Contributions calculated on supply work earnings
- Builds pension at same rate as permanent staff

Check with your agency whether they offer TPS membership.

## Opting Out

You can opt out of the Teachers' Pension, but it's rarely advisable:

### What You'd Lose

- 28.68% employer contribution (essentially free money)
- Death benefits (3Ã— salary lump sum)
- Ill health protection
- Guaranteed inflation-linked pension

<Callout type="warning" title="Think Very Carefully">
Opting out loses you the 28.68% employer contribution - a massive benefit. Most financial advisers strongly recommend staying in.
</Callout>

### If You Do Opt Out

- Complete an opt-out form via My Pension Online
- Your employer stops deducting contributions
- You can rejoin later
- Time out of the scheme means no pension growth
