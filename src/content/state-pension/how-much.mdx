## How is My State Pension Calculated?

Your State Pension amount depends on:

1. **Your National Insurance record** - How many qualifying years you have
2. **Which system you're on** - New or basic State Pension
3. **Any additional State Pension** - SERPS or State Second Pension (for basic State Pension)
4. **Contracted-out deductions** - If you were in a scheme that contracted out of the additional State Pension

### New State Pension Calculation

For the **new State Pension** (if you reached State Pension age on or after 6 April 2016):

- You need **35 qualifying years** for the full amount (Â£221.20/week)
- You need at least **10 qualifying years** to get anything
- Each qualifying year gives you 1/35th of the full amount

## What Affects Your State Pension Amount?

Several factors can affect how much State Pension you receive:

### 1. National Insurance Record

Your State Pension is based on National Insurance contributions and credits:

- **Working and earning** above Â£12,570/year (2024/25) gives you credits automatically
- **Receiving certain benefits** gives you National Insurance credits
- **Caring for children** under 12 while claiming Child Benefit gives you credits
- **Being a registered carer** can give you credits

### 2. Contracting Out

If you were "contracted out" of the additional State Pension (through a workplace pension), your State Pension may be reduced. This was common in public sector schemes and some private pensions before 2016.

<Callout type="tip" title="Check for Contracted-Out Deductions">
Your State Pension forecast will show if you have any contracted-out deductions. These reduce your State Pension because you were building up a workplace pension instead.
</Callout>

### 3. Gaps in Your Record

If you have gaps in your National Insurance record, you may be able to fill them by:

- Paying voluntary National Insurance contributions
- Claiming credits you're entitled to but haven't claimed
- Checking for errors in your record
