The **NHS Pension Scheme** is one of the most valuable benefits available to NHS employees. As a defined benefit pension, it provides a guaranteed income in retirement based on your salary and years of service - regardless of stock market performance.

This comprehensive guide explains everything you need to know about the NHS Pension, from contributions to retirement benefits.

## What is the NHS Pension Scheme?

The NHS Pension Scheme is a **defined benefit** (DB) pension available to NHS employees in England and Wales. It's administered by NHS Pensions, part of the NHS Business Services Authority (NHSBSA).

Key features include:

- **Guaranteed pension** based on your salary and service, not investment performance
- **Employer contributions** of 23.7% on top of your contributions
- **Tax relief** on your contributions at your marginal rate
- **Death benefits** for your dependants
- **Ill health protection** if you can't work

<Callout type="info" title="One of the Best Pensions Available">
The NHS Pension is often cited as one of the best pension schemes in the UK. The employer contributes 23.7% of your salary - far more than most private sector employers.
</Callout>

### The 2015 Scheme (Current)

Most NHS staff are now in the **2015 scheme**, which uses Career Average Revalued Earnings (CARE):

- Your pension builds up at **1/54th** of your pensionable pay each year
- Previous years' pension is increased by **CPI + 1.5%** annually
- Normal Pension Age is linked to your State Pension age

### Early Retirement

You can take your NHS Pension from age **55** (rising to 57 from 2028), but your pension will be reduced for early payment.

The reduction is approximately **5% per year** before your Normal Pension Age.

## McCloud Remedy

The **McCloud remedy** was introduced to address age discrimination in how members were moved to the 2015 scheme.

### What It Means

- Members who were in the 1995/2008 schemes on 31 March 2012 may be affected
- You'll get a choice about which scheme's benefits to receive for the "remedy period" (April 2015 - March 2022)
- NHS Pensions will contact affected members

<Callout type="info" title="Automatic Process">
You don't need to do anything now. NHS Pensions will contact you if you're affected and give you choices about your benefits.
</Callout>

## NHS Pension Benefits

### Retirement Benefits

When you retire, you receive:

1. **Annual pension** - Payable for life
2. **Optional lump sum** - Tax-free (by exchanging pension)
3. **Annual increases** - Pension increases with inflation (CPI)

### Death Benefits

The NHS Pension provides significant protection for your family:

- **Lump sum** - 2x pensionable pay if you die in service
- **Survivor's pension** - For your spouse, civil partner, or nominated partner
- **Children's pension** - For dependent children

### Ill Health Benefits

If you become too ill to work:

- **Tier 1**: Enhanced pension if you may work again
- **Tier 2**: Enhanced pension if you'll never work again
