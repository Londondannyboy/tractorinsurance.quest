**Alpha** is the current Civil Service pension scheme for most members. This guide explains how alpha works and what benefits you'll receive.

## What is the Alpha Scheme?

Alpha is a **Career Average Revalued Earnings** (CARE) pension scheme introduced in April 2015. It's available to:

- All new civil servants joining since April 2015
- Existing members who moved to alpha (following McCloud remedy)
- Staff at participating employers

## Early and Late Retirement

### Early Retirement

You can take alpha benefits from age 55 (57 from 2028) with reductions:

- Approximately **4.5-5% per year** before NPA
- Reduction is permanent

### Late Retirement

If you work past your NPA:

- Your pension increases by approximately **0.2% per week** delayed
- This is in addition to any extra pension earned from continued work

## Partial Retirement

Alpha allows **partial retirement**:

- Draw some or all of your pension
- Continue working and building more pension
- Reduce hours but maintain income
- Must be at least 55 (57 from 2028)
