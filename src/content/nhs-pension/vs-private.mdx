## Ill Health Benefits

### NHS Pension

If you become too ill to work:

- **Tier 1**: Enhanced pension if you may work again
- **Tier 2**: Significantly enhanced pension if you'll never work again

### Private Pension

- No ill-health benefits
- You'd access your pot early (with potential penalties)
- Would need separate income protection insurance

## When Private Pensions Might Be Better

In rare cases, private pensions could suit you better:

### Potential Advantages

1. **Control**: You choose investments and access timing
2. **Flexibility**: Various withdrawal options (not just annuity)
3. **Death before retirement**: Full pot passes to family
4. **Higher risk tolerance**: Potential for higher returns
5. **Tax-free cash**: 25% can be taken tax-free

### When to Consider Alternatives

- You're very close to retirement and want control
- You have significant other pension provision
- You're extremely risk-tolerant and investment-savvy
- You don't expect to live long (poor health)
- You want to leave maximum inheritance

<Callout type="warning" title="Get Advice First">
Before opting out of the NHS Pension for a private arrangement, always seek independent financial advice. The vast majority of people would be worse off.
</Callout>

## Should You Have Both?

Yes! Having additional private savings alongside the NHS Pension is sensible:

### Good Reasons for Additional Private Pension

- Boost retirement income above NHS Pension
- Different tax treatment options at retirement
- Earlier access (from age 55, 57 from 2028)
- More control over part of your retirement
- ISAs offer tax-free growth and withdrawal

<Callout type="tip" title="Best of Both Worlds">
Keep the NHS Pension for its guarantees and employer contribution. Add a private pension or ISA for extra flexibility and savings.
</Callout>
