## How Your Contribution is Calculated

### Step 1: Identify Your Pensionable Salary

**Pensionable salary** typically includes:

- Basic salary
- Some allowances (check with your department)
- Contractual payments

**Usually not included**:

- Overtime
- Expenses
- Performance bonuses

<Callout type="info" title="Check Your Payslip">
Your payslip shows your "pensionable pay" figure used to calculate contributions.
</Callout>

### Step 2: Find Your Contribution Tier

Look at the table above to find which tier your salary falls into.

### Step 3: Calculate Your Contribution

Multiply your pensionable salary by your tier's percentage rate.

## Tax Relief on Contributions

You receive **tax relief** on your contributions, reducing the real cost.

### How Tax Relief Works

## Opting Out

You can opt out, but it's rarely advisable:

### What You'd Lose

- Employer contribution of up to 30%
- Death benefits
- Ill health protection
- Guaranteed pension

<Callout type="warning" title="Think Carefully">
Opting out loses you massive employer contributions. Most advisers strongly recommend staying in.
</Callout>
