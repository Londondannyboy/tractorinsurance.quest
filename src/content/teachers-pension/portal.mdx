**My Pension Online** is the self-service portal for Teachers' Pension members. This guide shows you how to login, register, and use the key features.

## How to Login to My Pension Online

### Steps to Login

1. Go to teacherspensions.co.uk
2. Click "Member Login" (top right)
3. Enter your email address and password
4. Complete any security verification
5. Access your pension dashboard

<Callout type="tip" title="Forgotten Password?">
Click "Forgotten your password?" on the login page and follow the instructions to reset it. You'll receive an email with a reset link.
</Callout>

### Registration Steps

1. Click "Register" on the login page
2. Enter your Teacher Reference Number and National Insurance Number
3. Create a password (must meet security requirements)
4. Verify your email address
5. Login with your new credentials

<Callout type="info" title="Finding Your Teacher Reference Number">
Your Teacher Reference Number appears on payslips, pension statements, and correspondence from Teachers' Pensions. Contact them if you can't find it.
</Callout>

## Your Pension Estimate

The estimate section shows:

- Your current pension value
- Projected pension at Normal Pension Age
- Years of pensionable service
- Breakdown by scheme (if applicable)

<Callout type="info" title="Estimate Assumptions">
The estimate assumes you'll continue working and contributing until your Normal Pension Age. Your actual pension may differ if circumstances change.
</Callout>

## Annual Benefit Statement

Each year, Teachers' Pensions produces an Annual Benefit Statement showing:

- Your pension benefits as at 31 March
- Death benefits payable
- Your qualifying service
- Any PCLS (lump sum) options

Access via: **Documents > Annual Benefit Statement**

## Nominating Beneficiaries

You can nominate who receives your death benefits:

### Death Grant

- Lump sum paid if you die
- Nominate anyone (not limited to family)
- Can split between multiple people

### To Update Nominations

1. Login to My Pension Online
2. Go to "Nominations"
3. Add or edit beneficiaries
4. Save your changes

<Callout type="warning" title="Keep Nominations Updated">
Review your nominations after major life events (marriage, divorce, new children). Outdated nominations could mean benefits don't go where you intend.
</Callout>

## Requesting Retirement Quotes

Get estimates for different retirement scenarios:

1. Login to My Pension Online
2. Select "Retirement" or "Quotes"
3. Choose your preferred retirement date(s)
4. Request the quote
5. Download when ready (usually within days)

Quotes show:

- Pension at selected date
- Any early retirement reductions
- Lump sum options
- Tax-free cash choices

## Security Tips

### Protecting Your Account

1. **Use a strong, unique password** - Mix letters, numbers, symbols
2. **Don't share login details** - Even with family members
3. **Log out when finished** - Especially on shared devices
4. **Update email if it changes** - So you receive important notices
5. **Check for https** - Ensure the padlock icon is showing

<Callout type="warning" title="Beware of Scams">
Teachers' Pensions will never email you asking for your full password or bank details. Report suspicious emails to them.
</Callout>
