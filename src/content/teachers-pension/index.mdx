The **Teachers' Pension Scheme** (TPS) is one of the most valuable benefits available to teachers in England and Wales. As a defined benefit pension, it provides a guaranteed income in retirement based on your salary and years of service.

This comprehensive guide explains everything you need to know about the Teachers' Pension.

## What is the Teachers' Pension Scheme?

The Teachers' Pension Scheme is a **defined benefit** (DB) pension for teachers and other education staff in England and Wales. It's administered by Teachers' Pensions on behalf of the Department for Education.

Key features include:

- **Guaranteed pension** based on your salary and service
- **Employer contributions** of 28.68% on top of yours
- **Tax relief** on your contributions
- **Death benefits** for your dependants
- **Ill health protection** if you can't work

<Callout type="info" title="One of the Best Pensions Available">
The Teachers' Pension is one of the best pensions in the UK. With employer contributions of 28.68%, the total contribution can exceed 40% of salary - far more than most private sector schemes.
</Callout>

### The Current Scheme (2015)

Most teachers are now in the **Career Average Revalued Earnings** (CARE) scheme:

- Your pension builds up at **1/57th** of your pensionable earnings each year
- Previous years' pension is increased by **CPI + 1.6%** annually
- Normal Pension Age is linked to your State Pension age

### Early Retirement

You can take your Teachers' Pension from age **55** (rising to 57 from 2028), but your pension will be reduced for early payment.

The reduction is approximately **5% per year** before your Normal Pension Age.

## Teachers' Pension Benefits

### Retirement Benefits

When you retire, you receive:

1. **Annual pension** - Payable for life
2. **Optional lump sum** - Tax-free (by exchanging pension)
3. **Annual increases** - Pension increases with inflation (CPI)

### Death Benefits

The Teachers' Pension provides significant protection for your family:

- **Death grant** - 3Ã— pensionable salary if you die in service
- **Survivor's pension** - For your spouse, civil partner, or nominated partner
- **Children's pension** - For dependent children

### Ill Health Benefits

If you become too ill to work:

- **Total incapacity**: Enhanced pension if you can never work again
- **Partial incapacity**: Pension based on service to date

## McCloud Remedy for Teachers

The **McCloud remedy** addresses age discrimination in how teachers were moved to the 2015 scheme.

### What It Means

- Members who were in the old scheme on 31 March 2012 may be affected
- You'll get a choice about which scheme's benefits to receive for April 2015 - March 2022
- Teachers' Pensions will contact affected members

<Callout type="info" title="Automatic Process">
You don't need to do anything now. Teachers' Pensions will contact you if you're affected and give you choices about your benefits.
</Callout>
