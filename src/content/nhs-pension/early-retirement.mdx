If you retire before your NPA, your pension is **reduced** to account for the longer payment period.

<Callout type="warning" title="Reductions Are Permanent">
Early retirement reductions apply for life. Your pension doesn't increase to the full amount later - it remains reduced throughout retirement.
</Callout>

### How Reductions Are Calculated

The reduction factors are actuarially calculated to account for:

- More years of pension payments
- Fewer years of contributions/growth
- Life expectancy assumptions

## Partial Retirement Options

You don't have to fully retire. There are flexible options:

### Wind Down (Partial Retirement)

You can take some of your pension while continuing to work:

- Draw part of your pension (with early payment reductions)
- Continue working and building more pension
- Gradually transition to full retirement

<Callout type="info" title="Wind Down Rules">
You must reduce your pensionable pay by at least 10% to use wind down. The pension you draw is reduced for early payment.
</Callout>

### Retire and Return

You can fully retire, then return to NHS work:

- Take your full pension (with any early reductions)
- Return to NHS employment
- Build a new pension in the 2015 scheme
- Subject to annual allowance limits

### Step Down

Reduce your hours or role:

- Lower salary means less pension growth
- But maintains employment and income
- Can delay touching your pension

### Strategy: Staggered Retirement

You could:

1. Draw 1995 benefits at 60 (no reduction)
2. Draw 2008 benefits at 65 (no reduction)
3. Draw 2015 benefits at SPA (no reduction)

This avoids early retirement reductions but means waiting longer for some benefits.

### The Break-Even Point

If you retire early, you receive more payments but each is smaller. The break-even point is typically around **age 82-85**:

- Before that age: Early retirement gives you more total pension
- After that age: Waiting would have given you more total pension

<Callout type="tip" title="Think Long-Term">
Average life expectancy at 65 is about 20 years. If you're in good health, waiting for your full pension often pays off in total lifetime income.
</Callout>

## Early Retirement Protection

### Mental Health Officers

Some staff in specific roles have protected early retirement ages:

- Special class status (pre-1995)
- Mental health officer status

These protections are limited and closed to new entrants.

### Ill Health Retirement

If you're too ill to work, you may qualify for **ill health retirement**:

- **Tier 1**: Enhanced pension if you may work again someday
- **Tier 2**: Significantly enhanced pension if you'll never work again

This is different from voluntary early retirement - no early payment reductions apply.

## How to Apply for Early Retirement

1. **Check your benefits**: Log into the NHS Pension Member Hub
2. **Request a quote**: Get estimates for different retirement dates
3. **Consider your options**: Full retirement, wind down, or partial
4. **Notify your employer**: Usually 3-6 months notice required
5. **Complete forms**: Apply through NHS Pensions
6. **Receive your pension**: Payments start from your chosen date
