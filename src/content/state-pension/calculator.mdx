### Factors That Can Decrease Your Amount

- **Contracted-out periods** - Reduced if you were in a workplace scheme that contracted out
- **Fewer than 35 qualifying years** - Proportionally less
- **Gaps in your NI record** - Missing years mean less pension

## How to Increase Your State Pension

If your forecast is lower than you'd like, you may be able to increase it:

### 1. Fill Gaps with Voluntary Contributions

You can pay voluntary National Insurance (Class 3) to fill gaps from the past 6 years. This costs Â£17.45 per week (2024/25) per missing year.

**Is it worth it?** Generally yes if:
- You're close to retirement
- You have fewer than 35 qualifying years
- You'll live long enough to benefit (typically 3-4 years of pension to break even)

<ExternalLinkCard
  href="https://www.gov.uk/voluntary-national-insurance-contributions"
  title="Voluntary National Insurance contributions"
  source="GOV.UK"
  description="Find out about paying voluntary contributions"
  trusted={true}
/>

### 2. Claim Credits You're Entitled To

You may be missing credits you could claim for:
- Caring for someone 20+ hours per week
- Being unemployed and looking for work
- Illness or disability

### 3. Defer Your State Pension

Delaying your State Pension increases it by **1% for every 9 weeks** you defer (about 5.8% per year).
