The **NHS Pension Portal**, officially called the **Member Hub**, is your online gateway to managing your NHS Pension. This guide shows you how to access it and what you can do.

### How to Log In

1. Go to the NHS Pension Member Hub
2. Click "Sign in to the Member Hub"
3. Sign in with your **NHS login** (or create one)
4. Complete identity verification if required

<Callout type="info" title="First Time Access">
If this is your first time, you'll need to create an NHS login and verify your identity. This can be done using your passport, driving licence, or by answering security questions.
</Callout>

### 1. View Your Pension Estimate

The portal shows your **Total Reward Statement** including:

- Your current pension value
- Projected pension at Normal Pension Age
- Years of pensionable service
- Your contribution history

<Callout type="tip" title="Estimate vs Reality">
The portal estimate assumes you'll continue working until your Normal Pension Age. Your actual pension will differ if you retire early, reduce hours, or leave the NHS.
</Callout>

### 2. Annual Benefit Statement

Each year, NHS Pensions produces an **Annual Benefit Statement** (ABS) showing:

- Your pension benefits as at 31 March
- Death benefits payable
- Any contracted-out deductions
- Your qualifying service

Access your ABS through: **My pension > Documents > Annual Benefit Statement**

### 3. Update Personal Details

You can update:

- **Address**: Keep this current for important correspondence
- **Bank details**: For when you start receiving pension payments
- **Nomination of beneficiaries**: Who receives your death benefits

<Callout type="warning" title="Keep Details Updated">
Out-of-date contact details could mean you miss important correspondence about your pension, including McCloud remedy information.
</Callout>

### 4. Nominate Beneficiaries

You can nominate who receives your **lump sum death benefit**:

- Navigate to: **My pension > Nominations**
- Add beneficiaries with their details
- Specify percentage for each person
- Update anytime as circumstances change

<Callout type="important" title="Nomination Importance">
If you don't nominate, NHS Pensions decides who receives your lump sum. Nominating ensures your wishes are followed.
</Callout>

### 5. Request Retirement Quotes

When you're thinking about retirement, request a quote showing:

- Your pension at different retirement ages
- Lump sum options
- Early retirement reductions (if applicable)
- Impact of part-time work

**How to request**: My pension > Retirement > Request an estimate

### 6. McCloud Remedy Information

The portal now includes McCloud remedy information:

- Your **Remediable Service Statement** (when issued)
- Options for the remedy period
- Comparison of legacy vs reformed scheme benefits

If you've been in different scheme versions (1995, 2008, 2015), your statement shows:

- Benefits in each scheme separately
- Combined total pension value
- Different Normal Pension Ages for each part

### Contact NHS Pensions

If you can't resolve portal issues:

- **Phone**: 0300 330 1346
- **Email**: nhsbsa.pensionsmember@nhs.net
- **Post**: NHS Pensions, PO Box 2269, Bolton, BL6 9JS

<Callout type="info" title="Phone Hours">
NHS Pensions helpline is open Monday to Friday, 8am to 6pm. Lines are busiest on Mondays and at month end.
</Callout>

## Mobile Access

The Member Hub works on mobile devices but there's no dedicated app. For the best experience:

- Use a modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox)
- Ensure your device date/time is correct
- Keep your NHS login credentials secure

## Security Tips

### Protecting Your Account

1. **Use a strong password** - Mix letters, numbers, and symbols
2. **Don't share login details** - Even with family
3. **Log out when finished** - Especially on shared devices
4. **Check for HTTPS** - Ensure the padlock icon is showing
5. **Update contact details** - So you're notified of any issues

<Callout type="warning" title="Beware of Scams">
NHS Pensions will never email you asking for your full password or bank details. Be wary of any unexpected emails claiming to be from NHS Pensions.
</Callout>
