The **Civil Service Pension Scheme** is one of the most valuable pension schemes in the UK. As a defined benefit pension, it provides a guaranteed income in retirement based on your salary and years of service.

This comprehensive guide explains everything civil servants need to know about their pension.

## What is the Civil Service Pension?

The Civil Service Pension is a **defined benefit** (DB) pension scheme for UK civil servants and employees of participating employers. It's administered by **MyCSP** (My Civil Service Pension).

Key features include:

- **Guaranteed pension** based on your salary and service
- **Employer contributions** of up to 30.3% on top of yours
- **Tax relief** on your contributions
- **Death benefits** for your dependants
- **Ill health protection** if you can't work

<Callout type="info" title="One of the Best Pensions Available">
The Civil Service Pension is among the best in the UK. With employer contributions of up to 30.3%, the total contribution can reach nearly 40% of salary.
</Callout>

### Alpha Scheme (Current)

Most civil servants are now in **alpha**, a Career Average Revalued Earnings (CARE) scheme:

- Your pension builds up at **2.32%** of your pensionable earnings each year
- Previous years' pension is increased by **CPI** annually
- Normal Pension Age is linked to your State Pension age

### Early Retirement

You can take your Civil Service Pension from age **55** (rising to 57 from 2028), but your pension will be reduced for early payment.

The reduction is approximately **4.5-5% per year** before your Normal Pension Age.

## Civil Service Pension Benefits

### Retirement Benefits

When you retire, you receive:

1. **Annual pension** - Payable for life
2. **Optional lump sum** - Tax-free (by exchanging pension)
3. **Annual increases** - Pension increases with inflation (CPI)

### Death Benefits

The Civil Service Pension provides significant protection for your family:

- **Death grant** - Lump sum if you die in service (typically 2Ã— salary)
- **Survivor's pension** - For your spouse, civil partner, or nominated partner
- **Children's pension** - For dependent children

### Ill Health Benefits

If you become too ill to work:

- **Upper tier**: Enhanced pension if you have limited life expectancy
- **Lower tier**: Unreduced immediate pension

## McCloud Remedy

The **McCloud remedy** addresses age discrimination in how members were moved to alpha.

### What It Means

- Members who were in older schemes on 31 March 2012 may be affected
- You'll get a choice about which scheme's benefits to receive for April 2015 - March 2022
- MyCSP will contact affected members

<Callout type="info" title="Automatic Process">
You don't need to do anything now. MyCSP will contact you if you're affected.
</Callout>
