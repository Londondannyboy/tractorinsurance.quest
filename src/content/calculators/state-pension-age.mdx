## Future State Pension Age Increases

### Rise to 67 (2026-2028)

Currently being phased in:

- Affects those born on or after 6 April 1960
- Gradual increase over 2 years
- Everyone born after 5 March 1961 has SPA of 67

## Why State Pension Age Matters

### For Retirement Planning

Your State Pension age affects:

- When income starts
- How long your private savings need to last
- Early retirement calculations
- DB pension Normal Pension Age

### For Work Decisions

Consider:

- Can you work until State Pension age?
- Do you need to access private pensions earlier?
- How will your health affect work capacity?

## Early and Late Claims

### Can't Claim Early

Unlike private pensions, you **cannot claim State Pension early**. You must wait until your State Pension age.

### Can Defer

You can **defer** (delay) claiming:

- Pension increases by ~5.8% per year deferred
- No maximum deferral period
- Must live approximately 17-18 years after claiming to benefit
